## Project context

This project focused on building a production-ready CRM for a service business with operational needs that extended well beyond a standard lead board. The goal was not only to collect leads, but to manage the downstream steps that turn a customer inquiry into a usable operational record.

To protect client confidentiality, the case study uses anonymized language and avoids private company details. What matters here is the workflow shape and the kind of product thinking required to make the CRM genuinely useful.

## The challenge

The workflow needed to support several responsibilities at once:

- capture and organize leads from different sources
- keep records clean and reduce duplicate handling
- generate contracts and PDF documents from structured data
- manage media uploads tied to the record lifecycle
- support follow-up through WhatsApp and email processes
- connect external web form submissions into the CRM

This meant the system had to be more than a contact database. It needed to behave like operational software.

## What I built

The implementation centered on **custom Odoo CRM behavior** supported by Python logic, reporting workflows, and production deployment.

Key delivery areas included:

- tailored lead management flows designed around the company&apos;s sales and coordination needs
- contract generation and PDF report creation from structured CRM data
- duplicate detection logic to reduce fragmented records and manual cleanup
- media upload handling to keep important context attached to the right entity
- messaging-oriented workflows for WhatsApp and email follow-up
- integration of external form submissions into the CRM pipeline
- deployment to VPS infrastructure for production usage

## Product decisions that mattered

The technical work was important, but the most meaningful decisions were often about **workflow clarity**:

- which data needed to be captured early versus later in the process
- how to prevent record fragmentation when multiple inquiries referred to the same lead
- how to structure generated documents so they remained fast to create and consistent to review
- where automation genuinely removed admin work versus where manual review was still appropriate

Those choices are often what separate a pleasant demo from a system people trust every day.

## Outcome

The result was a CRM shaped around business operations rather than generic defaults. It brought lead handling, reporting, documents, and communication-related tasks into a more coherent flow and created a stronger foundation for ongoing operational work.

For future employers or clients, this project demonstrates a style of delivery that combines:

- frontend and backend implementation
- operational process analysis
- ERP or CRM customization
- workflow automation
- production deployment ownership

That combination is especially valuable for companies that need software to support messy real-world workflows, not just surface-level features.
